Lesson 9 : Its vs. It's, 'Tis

"Its" is the possessive of the pronoun "it." Note that there is no apostrophe. Example: Its appearance was misleading. "It's" is a contraction meaning "it is." Example: It's a long way to Tipperary. "It's" can also be a contraction meaning "it has." Example: It's been a long day. "'Tis" is also a contraction meaning "it is." Example: 'Tis seldom used in modern English.
