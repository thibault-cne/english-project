Lesson 3 : Singular and Plural Nouns 1

Definition: Singular means only one. Plural means more than one. In order to make a noun plural, it is usually only necessary to add s. However, there are many irregular nouns that add es. The rules for spelling plural nouns are based on the letters at the end of the word. The chart below breaks up the rules into categories so that they are easier to remember.

Rule Examples
Most nouns Add s to form the plural dog -> dogs
cat -> cats
bug -> bugs
Nouns ending in s, x, z, ch, sh Add es to form the plural. For words that end in z, add an extra z before the es. fox -> foxes
box -> boxes
buzz -> buzzes
quiz -> quizzes
beach -> beaches
Nouns ending in f or fe Some nouns ending in f or fe just add s. Sometimes it is necessary to change the f to a v. In that case, always end the word with es. roofs -> roofs
safe -> safes
shelf -> shelves
wife -> wives
